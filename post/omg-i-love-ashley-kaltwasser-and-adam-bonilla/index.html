OMG I love Ashley Kaltwasser and Adam Bonilla

Review for the Bikini and the Brain Podcast and Bikini Competition Tips

 Posted on January 27, 2020  |  6 minutes  |  1213 words  |  Lucy Rebekah  |  

I found about Ashley because of her side hustle(main hustle?) as a fitness model, not so much because of her IFBB bikini pro bodybuilding and Olympia accolades. Since I watch allll the glute workout videos, I watched her glute videos, and I thought she had an awesome attitude. She is so bubbly and motivated and fun. Her coach, Adam Bonilla, is super smart, sensible, and low stress.

They started this “Bikini and the Brain” podcast together towards the end of last year where they talk about their experiences with pro bikini bodybuilding competitions and address common questions and misconceptions.

Basically, the bikini bodybuilding competition is for ladies who are going for a very balanced, symmetrical physique, who look fit and toned, but don’t have significant muscle definition. It is that X shaped physique with a snatched waist.

Now I think most women who go to the gym have a list of goals of varying levels of specificity. For example my list looks something like this (in no particular order):

  • Be healthier
  • Be fitter / cardiovascular fitness
  • Burn off some of that Christmas weight
  • Low intensity cardio exercise for stress relief
  • Be stronger, general strength increase
  • Load bones to reduce osteoporosis risk
  • Look hotter
  • Bigger glutes
  • Improve terrible posture

Now “Look hotter” is kind of vague. How exactly does one do that? I mean “bigger glutes” and “improve posture” would probably help with this, but what else should I do? Do I just work out everything? That will make me more toned and I will have bigger muscles and look fitter? Right?

Should I be working out core? Would that tighten up and cinch my stomach? Can I spot reduce fat? Do I do cardio? Do I need to overeat to be able to “bulk” and build muscle? How long will it take to see results? What could be the problem if I’m not seeing results?

Well, as it turns out, bikini competitors are experts in “looking hotter”, and Adam and Ashley have been doing this for years, which means they have a ton of experience, and a sustainable, realistic approach. Even if you aren’t interested in competing, if you are a woman working out and wanting to body build or body sculpt, they have loads of advice.

This advice is dispensed in a fun, motivational way and peppered with lots of anecdotes and references to health studies and research.

I get that they are doing content marketing for Adam’s coaching company, Team Elite Physique. But it is damn good content, and I gotta respect the marketing skillz. Adam almost has me convinced to get his body assessment and waist trainer (to be fair, it is a better design than the average waist trainer), and Ashley has me wondering whether I should do a transformation bikini competition for fun!

Here is an incomplete list of some of the things I have learnt from their podcast:

Genetics and shape?

  • You can’t spot reduce fat.
  • Muscle tie ins and the overall structure of your body (shoulder/hips/waist, ‘X shape’) are what impact what you will look like with training and is determined by genetics. Depending on where the muscle “ties in” to the bone, you will get a different look. So for example, Ashley doesn’t train her lats because they tie into the bone super low and make her waist look thicker. Some people have a low calf tie-in (ahem, me), which can give a “cankle” look (no need to be rude guys lol :’(). This also means different people might have to train the same muscle different amounts to achieve the same kind of size.
  • Waist training (i.e. wearing a trainer for several hours each day) and stomach vacuums can influence your waist a lot
  • You probably don’t want to train core if you are going for a small waist
  • Training shoulders will make your waist look skinnier

Boob job?

  • There are top ten bikini competitors and Olympia winners who don’t have fake boobs. Many competitors use moulded cups and padding, boob tape etc
  • If you get an under muscle boob job and go heavy on the pectoral flies, it can cause your pectoral muscles to rip off and detach from your sternum (no, no, no, I cringed so hard listening to this). In general, an under muscle boob job can impede your ability to do chest exercises. Bikini competitors don’t usually train chest, but this is something to keep in mind if you are a chest training fan.
  • Ashley would not recommend a boob job for competitors unless they have strong reasons outside competing to get one

Progress

  • If you are not seeing progress it could be you are not working out hard enough, or you could be eating more calories than you realise. Stuff like creamer, sauces. Artificial sweetener packets can actually contain real sugar in order to have more volume. Ashley gets her packets online. Finally it could be hormone imbalance, this does happen.

Diet

  • Different people can have vastly different basal metabolic rate. This is the calories you burn at rest. If you have dieted for a long time, then you may have a lower basal metabolic rate as your body will have adapted. If you diet for a bikini competition to cut, this will slow down your metabolism as your body adapts to the lower amount of calories. It’s very important to recover and reset after a period of dieting to reverse some of this damage to your hormones and metabolism.
  • Number of meals doesn’t really make a significant difference for muscle growth or weight loss
  • Fasted cardio doesn’t really make a significant difference for weight loss
  • Type of diet doesn’t really make a significant difference for weight loss, but you need to find a diet you as an individual are able to stick to
  • Lots of people have food insensitivities which can cause them to be bloated so Adam doesn’t use macro diets.
  • “Bulking” is counterproductive. You wanna eat a small surplus at most.

Cardio

  • As you get fitter and your body adapts, you will burn less calories doing the same cardio exercise. This means you need to change it up using the FITT principle.
    • Frequency - increase the frequency
    • Intensity - increase the intensity
    • Time - increase the time
    • Type - change the type of cardio
  • High intensity interval training (HIIT) is naturally good for this as it is always upping the intensity. Adam gets all his competitors to do HIIT. HIIT is also good because it increases your metabolism even when you are not exercising.
  • Keep in mind, if you do a lot of cardio, you may end up compensating by being less active in your regular life, so try not to let cardio stop you going out, doing chores, etc, which you would normally do.

There is so much more, and I have only listened to half the podcasts they’ve done so far! This is a great podcast and I am excited to listen to more episodes.
