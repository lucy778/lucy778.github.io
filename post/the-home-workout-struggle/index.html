How to motivate yourself to work out at home?

The home workout struggle - why is it so monstrously hard?

 Posted on May 9, 2020  |  5 minutes  |  915 words  |  Lucy Rebekah  |  

I’m a lot luckier than some people when it comes to home gym equipment. I have some adjustable dumbbells already, and even the resistance bands I preordered online have arrived. But despite this, my workout routine has collapsed.

For me, gym wasn’t about doing something. It was about going somewhere. Just the act of going there makes the rest so much easier. You’re there, at the gym, to lift weights. You might as well lift weights. After all, what else can you do?

The way this plays out at home is I flop on the ground ( literally) after doing a set, lie there for minutes, and then eventually lose interest and wander off after 2-3 different exercises.

Anyway, on that bright note, let’s talk about strategies for fixing this.

The don’ts

90% of success is avoiding the pitfalls - Taleb (it’s not but I couldn’t find a quote and I wanted to start with the nos)

Don’t have dumbbells which flay the skin off your palms

The dumbbells I have have just a steel grip ( never buy this type!). This gets painful after a while. I do have calluses, but your body can only do so much. I ordered some PPE gloves and they have made deadlifting and grip strength training way easier. Whatever you do, don’t get fingerless gym gloves, they are useless for 90% of what you need them for.

Now with the don’ts out of the way, let’s get on to the dos.

The dos

Do set the bar so low that you can stumble over it half drunk

I like to set low expectations for my workouts as this makes it easier to get started. ‘I’ll just go there and do 20min walking on an inclined treadmill. ‘I’ll just start working my way through my list of exercises and see how far I get.

This approach doesn’t work at home. When you can easily abandon your workout partway through, it’s not going to work out.

So here is the plan. Pare the exercises down to the bare minimum, and pair them with an accompanying podcast or audiobook which is at least 40 min long, and which is interesting.

Here is my minimal basic 2-day split:

Upper

  • We start with the back as I want to prioritise it.
  • 3x sets banded rows
  • 3x sets banded face pulls
  • 3x sets push ups interspersed with stomach vacuums
  • 3x sets overhead press
  • 3x sets lateral shoulder raises
  • 3x biceps curl
  • 3x sets triceps skull crackers
  • 3x barbell twist grip exercise

Lower

  • We start with the glutes as I want to prioritise them:
  • 3x hip thrust, both legs
  • 3x split squat, both legs
  • 3x Romanian dead lift, single leg
  • 3x clam shells, each side
  • Face pulls :)
  • That’s it, calves can get shafted.

These are all with 9.5kg or 4.5kg dumb bells and a 36 kg barbell.

Writing this out made me realise something. Having to do unilateral, single sided exercises makes your workout time explode. But there’s no way I can load enough weight otherwise. So after I wrote it out, I cut out seated and standing calf raises and Nordic hamstring curls, which I‚Äôd initially included in the list above.

I’m hoping that following tiktok shuffle tutorials and long walks will be enough for my calves.

Bonus morning posture exercises which I probably won’t do but let’s be optimistic

  • Athleanx neck exercise and shoulder flexibility rolling movement
  • Stand with back against wall and lift arms upwards while pressing palms flat against wall

This only solves half the issue. The other issue is that I need something to listen to. I have been very interested in true crime over the past few years, but outside of a few favorites, I’m losing interest, and what if there isn’t a podcast out that day?
