Morphe x Pony 35I Icy Fantasy Palette is cute but not for everyone

 Posted on January 27, 2020  |  2 minutes  |  345 words  |  Lucy Rebekah  |  

I have never purchased a Morphe product before this palette because I always found the shade duplication off putting. It looks like they are moving away from that type of palette, since they are discontinuing a bunch of the palettes which are basically 35 shades of brown.

I have also never bought a Pony collaboration because none of them have really been that interesting.

The concept for this Icy Fantasy Palette is awesome though. It is totally in line with Pony’s soft romantic style. There are a lot of pastels and variety of shades, not the usual Morphe monochromatic dupe-filled palettes.

So how does the palette perform? The mattes are easy to blend and not chalky. However, they are sheer and not super pigmented. You need to build them up. This works fine for me but I am close to the lightest foundation shade. I would not recommend this for someone with medium to deep skin tone.

Also the shimmers are disappointing. This is odd to me, since people usually say Morphe shimmers are good, but I am definitely used to a more intense shimmer than these. I do not like the single glitter shade in the palette as glitters are not eye safe, so this has very limited use.

The shadows are long-lasting, longer lasting than e.g. my colourpop pressed shadows.

Would I recommend this palette? I think it is a cheap way of getting this whimsical / cute / dreamy colour story. It’s definitely not easy to find pastel colour palettes, as most companies seem to release only brights. However, I would not recommend this for anyone with medium or darker skin tone unfortunately.
